    Challenge questions for students

    • Why does a fixed pulley not reduce the force needed, even though it is still useful?
    • Predict the effort needed to lift 400 N with a 4-rope system at 80% efficiency, then check.
    • If you pull 6 m of rope and the load rises 1.5 m, how many supporting ropes are there?
    • Set efficiency to 50%. How much extra effort is needed compared with the ideal case?
    • In the Atwood machine, what happens to acceleration when m₁ = m₂?

    Assumptions (stated in the model)

    • Massless, inextensible rope; tension is uniform along it (ideal).
    • Pulleys ideal unless Real mode + efficiency < 100%.
    • Lifting at constant velocity (quasi-static) — net force ≈ 0 — so Effort = Required effort.
    • Velocity ratio VR = IMA = n for these rope-and-pulley systems.
    • Movable-pulley mass adds to the lifted load; rope-mass option adds full rope weight.
    • Atwood: frictionless, massless pulley; masses accelerate as a = (m₁−m₂)g/(m₁+m₂).
